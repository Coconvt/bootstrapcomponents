PlayStation One

Source: Sony, Sony PlayStation logo, en.wikipedia.org (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_(console)#/media/File:Playstation_logo_colour.svg)

The PS1 is the first PlayStation ever released by Sony. It was released in Japan December 3rd, 1994, and in North America September 9th, 1995. Its design was minimal. It was just a gray box with some detail around where you put the game disk. The second model of this console was almost the same but a little smaller and contained less detail. The PlayStation was designed by Sony Interactive Entertainment to compete with Nintendo’s Nintendo 64. Sony placed a strong emphasis on 3D polygon graphics when creating the console’s design. This led them to not include a dedicated processer for 2D graphics. The console was extremely popular thanks to its extensive game catalog. This catalog included games from popular franchises such as Gran Turismo, Crash Bandicoot, Tomb Raider, and Final Fantasy. All these games have had several sequels. One of the most recent ones, Final Fantasy XVI was announced in June of this year. Not only can this console play video games that were revolutionary at the time, but it can also play CD’s. The Asian model (SCPH-5903) could even play video CD’s. It could do all the things that a regular CD player could do. This includes playing songs from the CD in a programmed order, shuffle the playback order, and repeat either one song or the entire disc. To honor this legacy console, Sony released the PlayStation Classic on December 3rd, 2018. The purpose of this console was to emulate old PlayStation games. It featured 20 pre-installed games including Tekken 3, Final Fantasy VII, Jumping Flash!, and Syphon Filter.
